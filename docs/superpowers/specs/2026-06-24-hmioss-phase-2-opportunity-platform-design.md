# HMIOSS Phase 2 Opportunity Platform Design

**Date:** 2026-06-24

**Project:** International Hung Men Institute of Strategic Studies website

**Scope:** Phase 2 enhancement of the existing approved website

## Objective

Shift the website from an informational institute website into a partnership and opportunity platform while preserving the approved design system, page layout language, typography, spacing, and visual identity.

Phase 2 should make two priorities unmistakable:

1. Programme recruitment
2. Partnership development

After Phase 2, a visitor should leave understanding:

1. HMIOSS develops leaders.
2. HMIOSS creates partnerships.
3. HMIOSS provides real career pathways.
4. HMIOSS is more than an NGO.
5. HMIOSS connects education with real-world opportunities.

## Core Positioning

The website should consistently reinforce this narrative:

> HMIOSS is a strategic leadership and education institution developing future leaders through education, mentorship, research and community engagement.

This line should function as a recurring institutional narrative, not as repeated filler. It should appear in strategic places where it strengthens clarity:

- Homepage
- Programmes page
- Partners page
- Impact page

## Hard Constraints

- Preserve the current approved design system and premium visual identity.
- Do not redesign the website.
- Do not shift to a new aesthetic direction.
- Keep `/programmes` as the flagship programme landing page.
- Do not introduce dynamic programme detail pages in this phase.
- Do not introduce major architectural changes.
- Build new content structures so future phases can support:
  - additional programmes
  - additional academic partners
  - additional strategic partners
  - student success stories
  - programme detail pages

## Strategic Shift

### From

- institute information
- programme description
- general institutional messaging

### To

- programme opportunity
- leadership development
- career pathways
- partnership ecosystem
- academic and industry collaboration

The Dual Credential Programme should no longer read like a course brochure. It should operate as the flagship conversion asset of the website.

## Architecture Strategy

Phase 2 follows Approach 1:

- keep the current routes and design shell
- strengthen homepage conversion structure
- turn `/programmes` into the main flagship landing page
- add partnership-opportunity sections to both the homepage and partners page
- expand shared config structures for programmes and partnerships

This phase should improve conversion and future scalability without over-modeling the system.

## Experience Priorities

### Primary Objectives

- student enquiries
- partnership enquiries
- institutional credibility

### Secondary Objectives

- stronger narrative continuity between leadership, education, research, and impact
- better future extensibility for programme growth

## Homepage Design

### Homepage Role

The homepage must act as the first conversion layer for:

- prospective students
- prospective strategic partners
- institutions evaluating HMIOSS credibility

### Required Section Order

The new section order should be:

1. Hero
2. Authority / trust layer
3. Why HMIOSS
4. Launch Your Leadership Journey
5. Metrics / proof
6. Vision / institutional positioning
7. Partnership Opportunities summary
8. Final CTA block

### Launch Your Leadership Journey

This section must appear immediately after `Why HMIOSS` and before any metrics or proof sections.

It should position the Dual Credential Programme as:

- Leadership Development
- Career Development
- Industry Exposure
- Academic Qualification
- Professional Qualification

It should also frame the programme as:

- One Academic Qualification
- One Professional Qualification
- One Integrated Career Pathway

This section must not lead with modules or subjects.

It must lead with outcomes:

- Leadership Skills
- Industry Exposure
- Mentorship
- Professional Recognition
- Career Progression

### Why Join Pathway

Within or directly below the flagship homepage programme section, display the opportunity journey visually:

- Student
- Training
- Industry Exposure
- Leadership Development
- Career Opportunities

This should read as a progression pathway, not a brochure summary.

### Partnership Opportunities Summary

The homepage should include a summary version of the partnership opportunity section.

Categories:

- Universities
- Training Providers
- Industry Partners
- Defence & Security Partners
- Community Organisations
- CSR Partners

The homepage version should:

- explain that HMIOSS welcomes partnership development
- preview collaboration categories
- push users toward the full partners page

### Final Homepage Conversion Block

Add a dedicated CTA block:

> Ready to Begin Your Leadership Journey?

Buttons:

- Apply for Programme
- Become a Strategic Partner
- Contact HMIOSS

This block should be direct, high-intent, and conversion-first.

## Programmes Page Design

### Role

The Programmes page becomes the primary conversion page of the website and the flagship landing page for the Dual Credential Programme.

It should function as:

- a recruitment page
- a leadership development proposition
- a career pathway page
- a credibility asset

It should not function as a traditional course brochure.

### Positioning

The Dual Credential Programme should be presented through five value lenses:

- Leadership Development
- Career Development
- Industry Exposure
- Academic Qualification
- Professional Qualification

### Page Structure

Recommended section order:

1. Hero
2. Why This Programme
3. Career Pathways
4. Industry Exposure
5. Role of HMIOSS
6. Role of Genovasi
7. Entry Requirements
8. Apply Now

### Why This Programme

This section should explain why the programme matters in real-life terms:

- leadership formation
- employability
- professional identity
- dual-recognition value
- practical development

### Career Pathways

This section should make the career narrative clear and visual.

It should explain how participants move from learning into:

- leadership growth
- professional readiness
- industry familiarity
- long-term career progression

### Industry Exposure

This section should explain that the programme is connected to real-world environments and institutional ecosystems.

Examples of framing:

- exposure to professional environments
- applied institutional learning
- partner-linked experience
- operational awareness
- real-world context for leadership growth

### Role of HMIOSS

This section should clarify that HMIOSS provides:

- leadership development
- mentorship environment
- institutional identity
- community and service orientation
- strategic education framing

### Role of Genovasi

This section should clarify academic collaboration and qualification credibility.

It should frame Genovasi as the academic partner responsible for the academic qualification pathway and educational standards.

### Entry Requirements

This section should be:

- simple
- visible
- confidence-building
- non-intimidating

### Apply Now

The final section should be conversion-oriented, not informational.

It should help the user take the next step immediately.

Suggested CTA set:

- Apply for Programme
- Request Information
- Contact HMIOSS

## Partners Page Design

### Role

The Partners page should become a partnership development page, not only an institutional listing page.

### Core Goal

Explain how organisations can collaborate with HMIOSS and why that collaboration matters.

### Full Partnership Opportunities Section

This page should contain the full version of the partnership-opportunity structure.

Categories:

- Universities
- Training Providers
- Industry Partners
- Defence & Security Partners
- Community Organisations
- CSR Partners

For each category, explain:

- how organisations can collaborate
- what kind of partnership formats are possible
- what value the partnership creates
- how it supports talent and leadership development

### Partnership Narrative

The page should reinforce that HMIOSS is building a connected ecosystem between:

- education
- training
- industry
- defence and security
- community impact

## Shared Content Strategy

Phase 2 should extend structured content in a way that supports future growth without introducing heavy architecture.

### Programmes Config

Expand the programme content structure to support:

- flagship programme positioning
- outcome groups
- recruitment headlines
- pathway stages
- industry exposure copy
- HMIOSS role
- Genovasi role
- entry requirements
- CTA blocks

The data model should be able to grow into:

- multiple programmes
- future programme detail pages
- student success stories

### Partners Config

Introduce a dedicated partnership config structure that supports:

- homepage summary cards
- full partners page categories
- collaboration rationale
- future partner expansion

The data model should be able to grow into:

- additional academic partners
- additional strategic partners
- partnership opportunities by category

## Narrative Hierarchy

Throughout Phase 2, the order of emphasis should be:

1. Leadership development
2. Education
3. Career and partnership opportunity
4. Research
5. Community engagement

This order should influence:

- headings
- subheadings
- section sequencing
- CTA language
- summary copy

## SEO And Messaging Guidance

Phase 2 should strengthen page-level metadata for the most conversion-oriented pages:

- Homepage
- Programmes
- Partners

Descriptions should clearly communicate:

- leadership development
- education
- career pathways
- partnerships
- institutional credibility

## Success Criteria

Phase 2 is successful if:

- the homepage reads as a partnership and opportunity platform
- `/programmes` functions as the flagship landing page
- programme copy is outcome-led rather than brochure-led
- partnership opportunities are visible on both homepage and partners page
- the site feels more connected to real-world opportunity pathways
- HMIOSS is remembered as a leadership and education institution, not an association

## Non-Goals

Phase 2 will not include:

- dynamic programme detail pages
- student success story pages
- partner profile pages
- a visual redesign
- a CMS migration
- structural route expansion beyond the approved use of existing routes

## Post-Implementation Handoff Requirement

After implementation is complete, the final delivery should include:

- a page-by-page summary
- a visual review checklist
- deployment-readiness notes

## Summary

Phase 2 turns the HMIOSS website into a stronger opportunity and partnership platform by making programme recruitment and partnership development the dominant conversion goals, while keeping the approved design system and route structure intact.
